A look into Vancouver's geographic landscape

There are several beaches throughout the lower mainland, and mountains are visible to the north in almost every location across the city. That brings us to some of the most famous aspects of Vancouver’s culture, skiing. Ski resorts on various mountains are a big part of tourism in Vancouver. Vancouver is one of the only cities in Canada with warm temperatures all year round, while almost every other major city has blistering cold winters with less than –20 degrees. Vancouver is able to maintain the growth of palm trees which gives the city a sub tropical feel. The majority of British Columbia was largely made up from tectonic and volcanic activity which is why it is so mountainous. There are also a lot of islands surrounding Vancouver with the province's capital being on Vancouver Island. One of Vancouver's surrounding municipality's called Richmond is actually below the sea level and is sheltered by river banks and hills keeping it from flooding. Among the 75% of mountains, there is still some flat plains streching out into the Fraser Valley.
